Learning Objective

The game is intended to exemplify the "Tragedy of the Commons" nature of a joint acquisition program, where the lesson is that "Individually optimal decisions lead to collectively inferior solutions." It should show that, left unchecked, the collective custom feature demands of the stakeholder programs can require levels of effort and produce complexity that can overwhelm the development capability of the JPO, and even impact its ability to maintain its initial rate of development, causing the schedule to slip and miss the critical "need dates" of stakeholders, making them want to leave the program - but refusing to accept these demands can cause stakeholders to become disenchanted and try to leave the program (in favor of developing a custom, "siloed" system), thus undermining its viability, and when enough stakeholder programs leave, eventually leading to its irrelevance and/or cancellation.
