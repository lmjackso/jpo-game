Purpose of this Study

The purpose of the study is to investigate the way people behave in a joint program and perform to maximize their personal interest.

Procedure

The study will take approximately forty-five minutes to an hour. All study procedures will take place in SEI at Carnegie Mellon University. During this study, you will play a game in which you will try to release your product on time with all the capabilities promised to your customers and stockholders without exceeding your budget. The Game will have a finite number of rounds in which you will be asked to complete some questionnaires about demographic information, your culture, and your profession. You will make decisions in a multiplayer computer game to help your avatar succeed in a simulated organizational environment. You may also be asked to complete a questionnaire at the end of each game, which will assess your impression of the other players and the game in general.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life during normal group interactions. This may include boredom, discomfort, confusion, anger, and peer pressure. If you want to discontinue participation in the experiment at any time, you are free to do so.

Benefits

From your participation in this study, you may learn more about psychology, sociology, market economics, organizational behavior and group dynamics. Additionally, the knowledge gained from conducting this study may be of value to humanity.

Confidentiality

By participating in the study, you understand and agree that Carnegie Mellon University SEI may be required to disclose your consent form, data and other personally identifiable information as required by law, regulation, subpoena or court order. Otherwise, your confidentiality will be maintained in the following manner:

Your data and consent form will be kept separate. Your consent form will be stored in a locked location on Carnegie Mellon University SEI property and will not be disclosed to third parties. By participating, you understand and agree that the data and information gathered during this study may be used by Carnegie Mellon University SEI and published and/or disclosed by Carnegie Mellon University to others outside of Carnegie Mellon University.

However, your name, address, contact information and such publication or dissemination of the research data and/or results by Carnegie Mellon University. The researchers will take the following steps to protect participants' identities during this study : (1) Each participant will be assigned a number; (2) The researcher will record any data collected during the study by number, not by name; (3) Any original recordings or data files will be stored in a secured location accessed only by authorized researchers.

Rights

Your participation is voluntary. You are free to stop your participation at any point. Refusal to participate or withdrawal of your consent or discontinued participation in the study will not result in any penalty or loss of benefits or rights to which you might otherwise be entitled. The Principal Investigator may at his/her discretion remove you from the study for any of a number of reasons. In such an event, you will not suffer any penalty or loss of benefits or rights, which you might otherwise be entitled.
